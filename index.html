The Global Challenge We Face

The world today grapples with pressing issues: the escalating climate crisis, ongoing conflicts, and a growing trend of developed nations turning inwards. These challenges threaten not only our planet's delicate ecosystems but also the rich tapestry of human cultures and the well-being of vulnerable populations. My work is dedicated to addressing these fundamental threats with integrated, compassionate solutions.

Case Study: Resilience of the Paiwan Tribe, Taiwan

My master's thesis research on the Paiwan tribe in Taiwan exemplifies my commitment to integrated solutions. Following a typhoon-induced disaster that led to their displacement, I investigated the community's post-disaster recovery and resilience.

This involved understanding how land-based solutions contributed to food security and established long-term economic livelihoods, all while navigating structural constraints. This experience underscored the critical link between environmental events, cultural survival, and the adaptive resilience of human populations. It demonstrated that conservation and empowerment must work in tandem.
